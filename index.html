FAQ's

Determining whether your roof requires repair or replacement depends on several factors, including the age of the roof, the extent of damage, and the type of roofing material. For instance, asphalt shingle roofs typically last 20 to 30 years, while metal roofs can endure 50 years or more. If your roof is nearing the end of its expected lifespan and has significant damage, replacement might be more cost-effective. Conversely, minor issues like a few missing shingles may only require repairs.

Not all leaks necessitate a complete roof replacement. On relatively new roofs, isolated leaks caused by issues like damaged flashing or missing shingles can often be repaired effectively. However, if the roof has widespread damage or multiple leaks, especially in older roofs, a full replacement might be more appropriate.

The cost of roofing repair or installing a new roof system depends on various factors, including the pitch of your roof, the size of the area needing repair, the type of materials used, the condition of the existing roof, current market costs, the number of shingle layers, and the chosen roofing contractor. Obtaining multiple estimates can help you understand the potential costs and choose the best option for your situation.
